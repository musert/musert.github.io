Research

Selected Research Projects

My research interests span the areas of machine learning, semantic multimedia, multimodal learning, audio signal processing, and vision-language understanding, along with their applications in health, social systems, and IoT systems.

Prospective MSc/PhD Students OPEN

I am looking for highly motivated MSc/PhD students interested in broad areas of ML and AI.

Early Detection of Respiratory Diseases Related to COVID-19

Early detection of respiratory diseases related to COVID-19 using speech and cough analysis software, with integration into telehealth services. The project focused on machine learning-based audio analysis for health-related applications.

Researcher, TUBITAK EEEAG, Grant No. 120E117, 2020-2021

Health Speech Cough Audio Analysis Machine Learning

Fusion-Based Framework for Wireless Multimedia Sensor Networks

Design and development of a fusion-based framework for surveillance applications in wireless multimedia sensor networks. The project investigated visual and auditory data fusion for energy-efficient object recognition and intelligent sensing.

Researcher, TUBITAK EEEAG, Grant No. 114R082, 2015-2018

Multimedia Sensor Networks Data Fusion IoT Systems Surveillance

Semantic Content Extraction, Storage, and Querying of Video Data

Semantic content extraction, storage, and querying of visual, audio, and text data in videos. The project combined multimedia content understanding with semantic representation, indexing, object-oriented database modeling, and user-oriented semantic querying.

Researcher, TUBITAK EEEAG, Grant No. 109E014, 2009-2013

Semantic Multimedia Video Analysis Audio-Visual Data Semantic Querying Content Retrieval

Intelligent and Fuzzy Object-Oriented Database Modeling

Extension of a fuzzy object-oriented database model for multimedia applications. The work introduced a multimedia-specific conceptual database model, a fuzzy index structure for efficient access to video objects, and a knowledge-based system with an inference engine and knowledge base.

Visiting Researcher, TUBITAK EEEAG, Grant No. 106E012, 2008-2009

FOOMM Fuzzy Databases Multimedia Databases Knowledge-Based Systems

Structural and Semantic Analysis of Audio Content

Ph.D. research on structural and semantic analysis of audio content for content-based querying and browsing. The work focused on representing audio content using MPEG-7-compliant descriptors, constructing similarity matrices, and applying image-processing-based techniques for similarity analysis.

Ph.D. Thesis, Gazi University, Ankara, Turkiye, 2003-2006

Audio Signal Processing Semantic Audio MPEG-7 Content-Based Retrieval

Design and Implementation of an MPEG-7-Based Audio Database

Design and implementation of an MPEG-7-based audio database for structured audio description and retrieval. The work focused on low-level audio feature extraction, MPEG-7 metadata representation, and content-based audio querying.

Research Assistant, TUBITAK-BILTEN / METU, Ankara, Turkiye, 2004-2005

MPEG-7 Audio Metadata Audio Database Retrieval

Real-Time Digital Signal Processing, Classification, and Demodulation System

Industrial project on the development of a real-time digital signal processing, classification, and demodulation system. The implemented algorithms included phase difference tracking between receiver/transmitter sampling clocks and digital clock synchronization on the TMS320C6701 DSP platform.

Research Assistant, industrial project supported by TUBITAK-UEKAE, Baskent University, Ankara, Turkiye, 1999-2001

Digital Signal Processing TMS320C6701 Clock Synchronization Demodulation
